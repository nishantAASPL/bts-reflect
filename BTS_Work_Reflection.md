# BTS Work Reflection – Structured As-Is Mapping (With Executive Summary)

> You are helping me map what I actually do in my BTS role. Follow this structure strictly.

---

## Step 1 – Context

Ask:
- What is your current role/title on the project?
- What is the name of the project?

---

## Step 2 – Ask Only 3–4 Simple Questions

Keep them practical and easy to answer:

1. What kind of work do you actually do day-to-day?
2. Where do you spend most of your time in a typical week?
3. Who usually comes to you, and what do they need from you?
   - *(Example: Manager → status, Dev → logic clarification, Client → insights, etc.)*
4. Do you directly talk to the customer regularly, or does your manager handle that?
   - If you talk to the customer, what is the designation of the person you usually interact with?

> **Do not add more questions.**

---

## Step 3 – Interpret and Reflect Back

Based on responses:

- Write a short structured interpretation **(5–8 lines)**
- Identify the **primary work-type area** this represents
- Describe whether the role is:
  - Internal execution-focused
  - Internal coordination-focused
  - External stakeholder-facing
  - Decision-support facing

Then ask:
> *"Is this an accurate summary of this area of your work?"*

---

## Step 4 – Expansion Checkpoint *(Mandatory)*

After confirmation, ask:
> *"Is there any other major type of work you do that we haven't discussed yet?"*

**If the answer is yes:**
- Repeat **Step 2** and **Step 3** only for that new area
- Keep questions limited to **2–3 relevant ones**
- Reflect and confirm again
- Continue looping until the user says there are no additional major areas

---

## Final Output *(Only After All Areas Are Covered)*

Provide two sections:

---

### A. Executive Summary *(Short Report Style – 1 Page Tone)*

Include:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Overall nature of the role** | High-level summary of what the person does |
| **Core contribution pattern** | Key recurring outputs and activities |
| **Who depends on this person** | By type/level |
| **Level of external exposure** | Internal-only vs. client-facing |
| **Cognitive intensity profile** | High / Moderate / Low load areas |
| **Decision influence pattern** | Direct vs. indirect influence on decisions |
| **Observed operating mode** | Execution-heavy, insight-driven, coordination-driven, hybrid, etc. |

> Keep it structured, professional, and objective. **Do not convert this into a performance evaluation.**

---

#### 1. Work Distribution Table (% Split, Realistic)

**Overall Nature of the Role**

**Core Contribution Pattern**
- Delivering analysis, validation, and RCA that shape managerial and client decisions.
- Preparing SQL datasets and applying LLMs to generate structured insights.

**Who Depends on You**
- **Manager & Team Leads** → rely on you for validation, RCA, and analytical clarity.
- **Teammates** → depend on your data preparation and logic support.
- **Clients** *(indirectly, soon directly)* → consume insights and outputs shaped by your work.

**Level of External Exposure**
Currently limited, mediated through your manager. Expected to increase as you begin direct client interactions.

**Cognitive Intensity Profile**
- High analytical load (SQL, LLMs, RCA)
- Moderate coordination load (team clarifications, manager updates)

**Decision Influence Pattern**
Strong decision-support role: your outputs guide managerial and client choices. Influence is indirect but critical, as insights shape project direction.

**Observed Operating Mode**
Hybrid execution-driven and insight-driven: heavy technical execution balanced with analytical interpretation.
